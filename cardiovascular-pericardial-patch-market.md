<p><ul><li>Autologous</li><li>Xenograft</li><li>Synthetic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cardiovascular pericardial patch market comprises three main types: autologous, xenograft, and synthetic patches. Autologous patches are derived from the patient's own tissue, reducing rejection risks and promoting integration. Xenograft patches are sourced from animals, typically providing a biocompatible alternative for patients, though they may carry a higher risk of immune response. Synthetic patches, made from artificial materials, offer versatility and availability, but might face challenges related to tissue integration and long-term biocompatibility. Each type has unique benefits and considerations in clinical applications.</p></p>
