<p><p>The academic advising software market is primarily divided into two types: cloud-based and on-premise solutions. Cloud-based software allows institutions to access advising tools via the internet, offering flexibility, scalability, and easier updates. This type often entails lower upfront costs and beneficial collaborative features. In contrast, on-premise software is installed directly on the institution's servers, providing greater control over data management and security. However, it typically involves higher initial investments and ongoing maintenance responsibilities. Both types serve to enhance student advising efficiency and effectiveness.</p></p>
