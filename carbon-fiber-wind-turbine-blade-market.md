<p><p>The carbon fiber wind turbine blade market includes various manufacturing techniques, primarily hand lay-up molding and resin transfer molding. Hand lay-up molding involves manually placing layers of carbon fiber and resin to create the blade, allowing for versatile design and customization, but it can be labor-intensive. In contrast, resin transfer molding utilizes automated processes to inject resin into a closed mold containing carbon fiber, resulting in consistent quality and faster production rates. Both methods aim to enhance blade performance and durability.</p></p>
